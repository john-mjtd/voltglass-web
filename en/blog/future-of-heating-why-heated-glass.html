The Future of Heating: Why Heated Glass Is a Revolution in Architecture

Traditional radiators and underfloor heating are slowly giving way to new technologies. One of the most interesting innovations is heated glass, which is changing the way we think about building heating.

What Is Heated Glass?

Heated glass is specially treated glass with an integrated heating layer that generates radiant heat when connected to electricity. Unlike conventional heating systems that heat the air, heated glass emits infrared radiation that directly warms people and objects in the room.

VoltGlass® technology uses a patented microstructure that is completely invisible and integrated directly into the glass. This means that a window, facade, or glass wall can function as a full-fledged heating element without any visible components.

Advantages Over Traditional Systems

1. Space Savings

Without radiators and distribution systems, you gain more usable space. In modern architecture, where every square meter counts, this is a significant advantage. Interiors can be cleaner and more flexible.

2. Energy Efficiency

Heated glass generates heat directly where the greatest heat losses occur – in glazed surfaces. Instead of compensating for losses, it actively creates heat, leading to a significantly better energy balance for the building.

3. Healthier Environment

Radiant heat doesn't circulate through the air, so it doesn't stir up dust and allergens. For allergy sufferers and asthmatics, this is an ideal solution. Additionally, the even distribution of heat without cold spots increases overall comfort.

4. Aesthetics Without Compromise

Architects can design buildings with large glazed areas without worrying about heat loss. Heated glass is visually indistinguishable from regular glass, so the design remains clean and modern.

Where Is Heated Glass Used?

The applications are wide-ranging – from family homes to office buildings to special applications such as conservatories, skylights, or outdoor canopies where heated glass can ensure snow and ice melting.

  • New buildings: Complete replacement of traditional heating systems
  • Renovations: Glass replacement in existing frames
  • Commercial buildings: Glass facades, shop windows, atriums
  • Special applications: Anti-frost protection, anti-condensation

The Future Is Here

With growing demands for building energy efficiency and stricter standards, heated glass is becoming an increasingly attractive choice. The combination of energy savings, design flexibility, and a healthier environment makes it a technology with the potential to change the entire construction industry.
